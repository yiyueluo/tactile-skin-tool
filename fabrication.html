Manual Fabrication

Here describes the manual fabrication of a tactile sensing matrix. It adopts a standard sandwiching structure for the resistive sensing matrix, wherein a pressure-sensitive layer is positioned between two sets of electrodes (horizontal and vertical). The alignment of electrodes is manually performed on thin adhesive, which is then affixed to the middle pressure-sensitive layer. A thin plastic wrap serves as the outermost layer for insulation. Electrical connections are manually established between both sets of electrodes and ribbon cables, subsequently interfacing with the circuit for readout.
